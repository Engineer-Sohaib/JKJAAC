Core Committee

Leadership
Profiles

JKJAAC is deliberately structured as a collective leadership — not a one-man movement. Core committee members represent diverse geographic, professional, and social constituencies across all districts of AJK. Each has risked personal freedom and safety for the movement.

De Facto Leader / Chairman — Muzaffarabad
Shaukat Nawaz Mir

The most prominent face of JKJAAC, Shaukat Nawaz Mir has led negotiations and major rallies including the iconic address at Lal Chowk, Muzaffarabad. He served as chief negotiator in the October 2025 federal talks after being arrested in the May 2024 crackdown. Has consistently maintained that the movement holds no hostility toward Pakistan's armed forces while firmly demanding structural governance reforms. In January 2026, he declared further negotiations with the federal government "not possible" due to the trust deficit created by non-implementation.

Chief Spokesperson — Communications Lead
Hafeez Hamdani

Hafeez Hamdani has served as the articulate voice of JKJAAC in media interactions and press conferences throughout the movement. He has been pivotal in communicating the movement's peaceful, constitutional nature to both domestic and international audiences, countering government narratives. His clear messaging helped JKJAAC maintain public sympathy even during periods of violence.

Core Committee Member — Legal Affairs
Sardar Umar Nazir Kashmiri

Sardar Umar Nazir issued JKJAAC's landmark international appeal on October 2, 2025, invoking the UN Charter, UDHR, and ICCPR. He has been a key voice asserting that AJK remains a disputed territory under UN resolutions and that the crackdown on protests violates both Pakistani constitutional guarantees and international humanitarian law. A critical bridge between JKJAAC and global diaspora advocacy.

Senior Leader / International Envoy — Muzaffarabad
Amjad Ali Advocate

A practicing advocate in Muzaffarabad, Amjad Ali has been JKJAAC's primary international spokesperson, including in a widely-covered 2025 UK visit where he explained the movement's constitutional basis. He confirmed that the movement's name "Jammu Kashmir" rather than "Azad Kashmir" was a deliberate choice reflecting its potential to expand region-wide, including to Gilgit-Baltistan and beyond. Self-funded and independent, he has emphasized: "These demands are not acts of rebellion, but reform-oriented proposals."

Founding Member — AJK-Wide
Imtiaz Aslam

One of the original founding members of JKJAAC, Imtiaz Aslam has strongly emphasized the movement's self-funded, independent character. He has directly challenged government allegations of foreign (specifically Indian) backing, stating: "Our fight is not with the state of Pakistan. We are only arguing against the corrupt rule of the current government here." A key figure in establishing JKJAAC's credibility with ordinary citizens skeptical of political movements.

Government Recognition

Leaders Who
Sacrificed

JKJAAC leadership has faced repeated arrests, legal persecution, and personal threats. In May 2024, around 70 activists were arrested overnight including Shaukat Nawaz Mir. This only inflamed public anger and proved counterproductive for the government.

May 8–9, 2024 — Mass Arrests

Around 70 JKJAAC activists arrested overnight across Muzaffarabad and Mirpur divisions, including Shaukat Nawaz Mir and multiple core committee members. The crackdown severely backfired, triggering the most intense protests in AJK's recent history and ultimately forcing the ₨23 billion relief package.

September 2025 — Communications Blackout

Pakistan's Ministry of Interior ordered mobile and internet services suspended across major parts of AJK on September 28–29, 2025, specifically to prevent JKJAAC from coordinating the announced lockdown. Despite the blackout, hundreds of thousands mobilized — a testament to the leadership's organizational capacity.

192 FIRs Filed — Later Largely Withdrawn

192 First Information Reports (FIRs) were filed against JKJAAC activists across the 2024 and 2025 protests. The October 2025 agreement included withdrawal of 177 of these FIRs, a major victory for the movement's legal standing and a recognition of the illegitimacy of the prosecutions.

International Appeal — October 2025

Sardar Umar Nazir Kashmiri issued a formal international appeal on October 2, 2025 invoking the UN Charter, UDHR, and ICCPR — marking JKJAAC's first formal engagement with international human rights frameworks and gaining coverage from diaspora media worldwide.

Organizational Structure

A Movement Built on
Collective Leadership

We are not a traditional organization with a single leader at the top. Instead, we are a dynamic network built on the principles of collective leadership. Our structure is decentralized and participatory, distributing power and decision-making across the group. By valuing every voice and fostering shared responsibility, we ensure that our direction is shaped by the collective wisdom of our members, not a single hierarchy. This approach makes us more agile, resilient, and deeply aligned with our mission.

Collective Decision-Making

No single leader can unilaterally decide JKJAAC's positions. All major actions require collective committee agreement — preventing individual co-optation by government.

Diaspora Network

Nearly half of AJK's population lives overseas — primarily in the UK. JKJAAC's diaspora network, particularly in Birmingham and London, provides crucial advocacy and financial support.

Social Media Strategy

Social media has been central to JKJAAC's organizing — allowing its message to reach every corner of AJK without distortion, even when traditional media aligned with government narratives.
