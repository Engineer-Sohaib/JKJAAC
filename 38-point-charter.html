38 Demands

JKJAAC is deliberately structured as a collective leadership — not a one-man movement. Core committee members represent diverse geographic, professional, and social constituencies across all districts of AJK. Each has risked personal freedom and safety for the movement.

A — Core Structural & Political Demands
01

Elimination of Elite Privileges

Immediate elimination of expensive perks, luxuries, and all privileges enjoyed by AJK ministers, lawmakers, bureaucrats, and judges. Resources must serve the people, not the entrenched elite.

✓ Implemented (Oct 2025)
02

Abolition of 12 Refugee Legislative Seats

Termination of the 12 reserved seats in the AJK Legislative Assembly for Kashmiri migrants settled in Pakistan — tools of political patronage that create a powerful, unaccountable bloc representing ~20% of assembly seats.

Committee Formed — Implementation Disputed
03

Cabinet Size Reduction

Reduction of the ministerial cabinet to a maximum of 20 members including advisors and special assistants. A bloated cabinet wastes public resources on political patronage.

✓ Implemented
04

Local Government Empowerment

Implementation of the Local Government Act of 1990 to ensure meaningful grassroots democracy. Full transfer of powers, funds, and functions to local government bodies across all AJK districts.

Partial Progress
05

Restoration of Student Unions

Lifting of restrictions on student unions in AJK universities and colleges, with transparent elections conducted. Student unions are a constitutional right and essential for democratic development.

✓ Implemented
06

Independent & Across-the-Board Accountability

Establishment of genuine, independent accountability mechanisms that apply to all public officeholders including politicians, bureaucrats, judiciary, and security establishment personnel in AJK.

Pending Implementation
B — Economic & Resource Rights
07

Electricity Tariffs Based on Local Production Costs

Electricity tariff for AJK must be determined from the actual cost of production at the Mangla Hydropower Project — not inflated national grid rates. AJK generates Pakistan's power but pays the highest bills.

✓ Implemented (May 2024)
08

Subsidized Wheat & Flour Prices

Wheat flour price to be maintained at subsidized rate (₨2,000/40kg or less) equivalent to or better than rates in Gilgit-Baltistan. Wheat subsidy to be permanent, not temporary.

✓ Implemented (May 2024)
09

Full Resource Royalties for AJK

AJK must receive its full share of royalties from natural resources — including hydropower, minerals, and forests — generated within its territory. Current arrangements constitute economic exploitation.

Pending
10

Tax Reforms & Exemptions

Reform of the tax structure to remove excessive and unjust levies on AJK residents. Given AJK's disputed status and resource contribution, citizens should not be subject to the same tax burden as mainland Pakistan.

✓ Implemented (FBR ATL Integration 2026)
11

Infrastructure Development Fund

Establishment of a dedicated federal infrastructure development fund for AJK proportional to its resource contributions. Roads, bridges, and public infrastructure have been chronically underfunded relative to AJK's economic contribution.

✓ Implemented
12

Restrictions on Timber Cutting & Forest Industry

Practical restrictions on tree cutting and legislation to revitalize the local wood industry. AJK's forests are being depleted without proportional economic benefit to local communities.

✓ Implemented
C — Social Rights & Human Development
13

Free Healthcare — Health Cards Restored

Restoration of the Sehat Sahulat health card program and expansion to cover all AJK residents with comprehensive, free medical care. The withdrawal of health cards was declared a direct violation of the October 2025 agreement.

Disputed — JKJAAC says not restored
14

Free Education at All Levels

Provision of free, quality education from primary through university level in AJK. Given the region's high literacy rate and educated diaspora, investment in education is both a right and an economic imperative.

✓ Implemented
15

Fixed Disability Quotas in Government Jobs

Mandatory quotas for persons with disabilities across all government departments and public sector institutions in AJK. Full implementation of existing disability rights legislation.

✓ Implemented
16

Compensation for 2024–2025 Martyrs & Injured

Monetary benefits equivalent to Law Enforcement Agencies for every protester killed. Government jobs for family members. ₨1 million compensation per injured person. Over ₨120 million disbursed by early 2026.

Partially Paid — Ongoing
17

Restoration of Internet & Mobile Services

Permanent guarantee of uninterrupted internet and mobile services in AJK. The government's ability to cut communications at will — as done in September 2025 — is a fundamental violation of citizens' rights.

Unresolved — Major Controversy
18

Reinstatement of Suspended Employees

Reinstatement of all government employees suspended for participating in or supporting the JKJAAC movement. No worker should face professional retaliation for exercising constitutional rights.

✓ Implemented (Oct 2025)

The full charter contains 38 demands across additional categories including environmental rights, judicial reform, and diaspora representation.
