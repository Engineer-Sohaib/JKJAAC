Our Mission

Fighting for Economic Justice and Rights

We are dedicated to challenging the political status quo, demanding economic justice, and ensuring the end of systemic exploitation and administrative neglect for the people of Azad Jammu & Kashmir — through peaceful, constitutional, and persistent civil action.

AJK generates Pakistan's hydroelectric power yet pays the highest electricity bills. We demand tariffs based on actual local production costs from Mangla Dam — not inflated national rates that drain the people.

1
AJK generates Pakistan's hydroelectric power yet pays the highest electricity bills. We demand tariffs based on actual local production costs from Mangla Dam — not inflated national rates that drain the people.
2
We demand abolition of all perks and privileges for ministers, bureaucrats, and judges. The cabinet must be capped at 20 members. Resources belong to the people, not the entrenched elite.
3
From restoring student unions to demanding full resource royalties, JKJAAC fights to ensure the people of Azad Kashmir enjoy their rightful constitutional, democratic, and human rights as citizens, not subjects.
4
All human rights violations must end. Freedom of speech and movement must be restored immediately.
5
The UN should facilitate free, fair elections for a representative Council — and a plebiscite for final status.

Our Timeline

How We Won Change

Step 01
Coalition Consolidates in Rawalakot
A sit-in at a flour depot in Rawalakot becomes the nucleus. Multiple local committees unite into JKJAAC by September 2023.
Step 02
₨23 Billion Relief Secured
Six days of mass protests force PM Shehbaz Sharif to announce ₨23 billion in federal relief. Wheat prices cut, electricity tariffs slashed.
Step 03
10 Martyrs — Internet Blackout
After 10 martyrs, a nationwide lockdown, and total internet blackout, the movement intensified pressure on the Federal Government.
Step 04
Historic Agreement Signed — 31/37 Fulfilled
October 4 accord signed. Federal Minister Amir Muqam confirms 31 of 37 agreement points implemented as of February 2026.
