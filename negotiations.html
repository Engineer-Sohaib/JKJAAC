Preamble

The 2025 Agreement:
A People's Victory

After five days of violent unrest, 10 confirmed martyrs, an internet blackout, and 13 hours of marathon negotiations on October 4, 2025, a high-level federal delegation reached a preliminary agreement with the JKJAAC in Muzaffarabad. Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif hailed the agreement as a resolution to "buried conspiracies." JKJAAC declared it a people's victory achieved through sacrifice and steadfast resistance. By February 2026, Federal Minister Amir Muqam confirmed 31 out of 37 points had been implemented — though JKJAAC disputes this characterization on several key points.

Following five days of violent unrest that tragically resulted in 10 confirmed martyrs and an internet blackout, a pivotal moment occurred on October 4, 2025. After 13 hours of marathon negotiations in Muzaffarabad, a high-level federal delegation reached a preliminary agreement with the JKJAAC. The resolution was met with contrasting, yet significant, reactions: Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif hailed it as a move that would finally "bury conspiracies," while the JKJAAC declared it a resounding "people's victory," born from their sacrifice and steadfast resistance.

The implementation of the 37-point agreement has since become a point of contention. By February 2026, Federal Minister Amir Muqam announced that 31 of the points had been successfully implemented, signaling progress from the government's perspective. However, this claim is directly disputed by the JKJAAC, which challenges the characterization of implementation on several key points, indicating that the underlying tensions and the full realization of the "people's victory" remain unresolved.

Negotiation History

Three Rounds
of Talks

The path to the October 2025 agreement was paved with failed negotiations, broken promises, and government bad faith. Understanding this history is essential to understanding why JKJAAC continues to press for full implementation.

Round 1 — November 2023
AJK Government Assurances
JKJAAC suspends protests after the AJK government provides assurances to address core concerns. No formal agreement is signed. The government fails to implement any substantive changes within the agreed timeframe. This breach of faith directly triggers the 2024 mass uprising.
Outcome: Failed — No implementation
Round 2 — December 2024 (Kohala)
Kohala Agreement — 12 Points
After the May 2024 victory, JKJAAC and the government meet in Kohala and agree on implementation of 12 key demands by June 30, 2025. The government implements only one demand (resumption of a major delayed project). JKJAAC is given a six-month extension. At the end of the extension, it presents the expanded 38-Point
Charter. Outcome: Partial — 1 of 12 demands met
Round 3 — October 4, 2025
Historic Federal Agreement
After the 2025 uprising claims 10 lives, a federal delegation engages in 13-hour marathon negotiations with JKJAAC in Muzaffarabad. A comprehensive agreement covering compensation, legal relief, governance reforms, and economic measures is signed. The most substantial JKJAAC-government accord to date.
Outcome: 31 of 37 points confirmed by Feb 2026

The October 4, 2025 Agreement

Key Points of the
Historic Accord

The agreement covered compensation for martyrs and injured, legal relief for arrested protesters, structural governance reforms, economic measures, and long-term institutional changes. It represented JKJAAC's most comprehensive victory to date.

01

Compensation for Martyrs

Monetary benefits equivalent to Law Enforcement Agencies granted for each protester killed. Government jobs provided to family members. Financial compensation for bereaved families — implementation ongoing as of March 2026.

02

₨1 Million Per Injured Protester

₨1 million compensation for every individual injured during the 2025 protests. Medical expenses to be covered by the government. Total payments exceeding ₨120 million disbursed by early 2026 — though JKJAAC says many families remain unpaid.

03

Withdrawal of 177 FIRs

Withdrawal of 177 out of 192 First Information Reports registered against JKJAAC protesters during the 2024 and 2025 uprisings. Release of detained protesters. 15 FIRs related to specific violent incidents to be reviewed separately.

04

Reinstatement of Suspended Employees

All government employees suspended for participation in or support of JKJAAC protests to be reinstated with back pay. Reinstatement confirmed implemented as of October 2025.

05

Cabinet Reduction to 20 Members

AJK cabinet including advisors and special assistants to be reduced to maximum 20 members. This demand — central to JKJAAC's anti-elite stance — was confirmed implemented in October 2025.

06

Refugee Seats Legal Committee

Formation of a committee of legal experts to review and propose abolition of the 12 refugee legislative seats. On December 29, 2025, Pakistan issued a notification announcing formation of a new committee — but JKJAAC contests the appointment of a refugee as Public Accounts Committee chairman as a violation of the agreement's spirit.

07

Restoration of Health Cards & Internet

Agreement to restore the Sehat Sahulat health card program for all AJK residents and to restore mobile and internet services. JKJAAC states as of January 2026 that neither has been restored — representing the movement's most significant ongoing grievance.

08

Student Union Restoration

Immediate restoration of student unions and conduct of elections in all AJK universities and colleges. Confirmed implemented. AJK students now hold their first democratic union elections since the era of restrictions.
